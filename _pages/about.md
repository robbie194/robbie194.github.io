I am a Ph.D. student in Electrical and Electronic Engineering at [The University of Hong Kong](https://www.hku.hk/), working on computational imaging, health sensing, neuromorphic imaging, and AI-powered visual systems.

Before joining HKU, I worked as an algorithm engineer on computer vision and imaging systems. My industrial experience spans smartphone camera object detection and tracking, contactless in-cabin health monitoring, semiconductor virtual metrology, and wafer map image classification. These projects involved model training and quantization, algorithm integration, signal processing, edge/cloud deployment, and cross-team product delivery.

I received my M.E. in Optical Engineering from [Nanjing University](https://www.nju.edu.cn/) and my B.E. in Optoelectronic Information Science and Engineering from [Shandong University](https://www.sdu.edu.cn/). My earlier research focused on Fourier ptychographic microscopy, super-resolution microscopy, and fiber-based single-pixel imaging.

My current interests sit at the intersection of optical imaging, computer vision, and deployable AI systems:

- Computational imaging and inverse problems
- Neuromorphic and event-based imaging
- Contactless physiological sensing and health monitoring
- Optical system design with learning-based reconstruction
- Real-time vision algorithms for embedded and edge platforms
